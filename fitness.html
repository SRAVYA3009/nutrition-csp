Find Time to Exercise

Exercise in the Morning

  • Mood Boosting

    • Exercising as soon as you wake up is excuse-proof. Keep your exercise clothes and shoes by the bed, so you're ready when the sun rises.Studies show that people who work out in the morning are more likely to stick with exercise. If you wait until later, too many things can get in the way. Do it before the sun comes up.

  • Exercise Benefits

    • The idea that you have to spend 30 or 60 minutes working out is a thing of the past as science shows all movement counts. In fact, research shows several 10-minute workouts throughout the day may be more beneficial than the same amount of exercise in one session. One study found that diabetics who walked briskly for 10 minutes after a meal had better blood sugar control than those who exercised for 30 minutes straight. If necessary, break your workouts up into smaller chunks.

Staying Fit on a Busy Schedule

  • Walk/Bike to work

    • If you have the advantage of living relatively close to your work or school, consider biking or walking. Both are great exercise and would be a great way to start the day. You will also be saving money and reducing your carbon footprint.

  • Accomplish other tasks while working out

    • If you have a conference call that you know will take a while, put on some headphones and carry the call with you as you go for a run, walk, or gym session. Listening to podcasts or audiobooks is another great way to kill two birds with one stone as you work out.

  • Do workouts with family or friends

    • If you are worried about losing time with family or friends due to your fitness regimen, encourage them to work out with you. Joining a fitness class or just doing workouts together can be great fun and a great way to spend time together.



Developing a Successful Exercise Habit

  • Make a promise to yourself

    • Your health is your own business, and while you may need to trust that the health-care workers you work with know what they're doing, you are the one who has final say over your body. No one can take away that right. So own your body, own that it is part of who you are, and if you're determined to make exercise a part of your life, own that this determination is yours, and yours alone.

  • Take breaks, but don't break the habit!

    • It might seem counter-intuitive, but it's important to set aside some days in the week for rest. If you're completely able-bodied and relatively healthy, perhaps one day a week of rest will be enough for your body to recover. You don't have to stop exercising completely on those days, but you do need to switch things around.

Improving Flexibility

  • Decreased Risk of Injuries

    • Becoming more flexible is one of the best ways you can prevent injury during exercise or daily life. Flexibility is important for your joints to achieve their full range of motion while engaging in an activity. Without practicing flexibility, you can put too much strain and pressure on your joints.

  • Circulation and Heart Health

    • Stretching and flexibility improves your circulation, which is necessary to ensure nutrients and oxygen are able to flow through your body. Good circulation ensures your heart is receiving plentiful amounts of oxygen. Stretching can also increase your arterial flexibility and lower blood pressure.
